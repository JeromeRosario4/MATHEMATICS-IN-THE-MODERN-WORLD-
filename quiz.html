Binary Logic — Quick Quiz

1.
What numbering system do computers use to perform all operations?
A. Decimal
B. Binary
C. Octal
D. Hexadecimal
Answer: B. Binary
2.
What digits are used in the binary number system?
A. 0–9
B. 0–7
C. 0 and 1
D. 1–9
Answer: C. 0 and 1
3.
What does each digit in a binary number represent?
A. A power of 2
B. A power of 10
C. A group of four bits
D. A voltage signal only
Answer: A. A power of 2
4.
What are the two voltage levels in digital logic typically referred to as?
A. High and low
B. Positive and neutral
C. Full and empty
D. Light and dark
Answer: A. High and low
5.
What electronic component is used to create logic gates?
A. Switches
B. Capacitors
C. Transistors
D. Diodes
Answer: C. Transistors
6.
What does an AND gate output when both inputs are 1?
A. 0
B. 1
C. Undefined
D. Alternating signal
Answer: B. 1
7.
What is the output of an AND gate when both inputs are 0?
A. 1
B. 0
C. Undefined
D. Random
Answer: B. 0
8.
What is the output of an OR gate when both inputs are 0?
A. 1
B. 0
C. Undefined
D. Alternating signal
Answer: B. 0
9.
What is the output of an OR gate when at least one input is 1?
A. 0
B. 1
C. Alternating
D. Undefined
Answer: B. 1
10.
What does an XOR (Exclusive OR) gate output when both inputs are the same?
A. 1
B. 0
C. Random
D. Undefined
Answer: B. 0
11.
What is the XOR gate’s function rule?
A. Outputs 1 if both inputs are 1
B. Outputs 1 if inputs are different
C. Outputs 1 if both inputs are 0
D. Outputs 1 if any input is 0
Answer: B. Outputs 1 if inputs are different
12.
In a Half Adder, what does the XOR gate represent?
A. Carry output
B. Sum output
C. Overflow bit
D. Borrow bit
Answer: B. Sum output
13.
In a Half Adder, what does the AND gate represent?
A. Carry output
B. Sum output
C. Overflow bit
D. Reset signal
Answer: A. Carry output
14.
What is the formula for a Half Adder?
A. Sum = A OR B; Carry = A AND B
B. Sum = A XOR B; Carry = A AND B
C. Sum = A AND B; Carry = A XOR B
D. Sum = A NOR B; Carry = A NAND B
Answer: B. Sum = A XOR B; Carry = A AND B
15.
What limitation does a Half Adder have?
A. It cannot process binary inputs
B. It cannot handle carry inputs from previous additions
C. It only works with decimal numbers
D. It produces no output
Answer: B. It cannot handle carry inputs from previous additions
16.
What is the main improvement of a Full Adder over a Half Adder?
A. It uses fewer gates
B. It can add three bits (A, B, and Carry-in)
C. It only uses XOR gates
D. It ignores carry input
Answer: B. It can add three bits (A, B, and Carry-in)
17.
What logic gates are used to build a Full Adder?
A. Two XOR, two AND, and one OR gate
B. One OR, one XOR, and one NOT gate
C. Two AND and two OR gates
D. Three NAND gates only
Answer: A. Two XOR, two AND, and one OR gate
18.
What is the Sum output equation for a Full Adder?
A. Sum = (A XOR B) XOR Cin
B. Sum = (A AND B) OR Cin
C. Sum = A OR B OR Cin
D. Sum = (A XNOR B) AND Cin
Answer: A. Sum = (A XOR B) XOR Cin
19.
What is the Carry output equation for a Full Adder?
A. Cout = (A AND B) OR (Cin AND (A XOR B))
B. Cout = (A OR B) AND Cin
C. Cout = (A XOR B) AND Cin
D. Cout = (A NOR B) OR Cin
Answer: A. Cout = (A AND B) OR (Cin AND (A XOR B))
20.
What happens in a multi-bit binary addition when a carry is generated from one bit position?
A. It is ignored
B. It becomes the carry input for the next bit
C. It resets the sum
D. It stops the operation
Answer: B. It becomes the carry input for the next bit
